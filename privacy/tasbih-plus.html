Tasbih+ — Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2 July 2026
App: Tasbih+ (Islamic companion — dhikr counter, prayer times, Qibla, Quran)
Publisher: Reimel Ltd, 159a High Street, Hornchurch, England, RM11 3YD

Tasbih+ is an offline Islamic companion app. This policy explains exactly what it does with information on your device. In short: the app collects nothing about you and sends nothing to us or to anyone else.

Data we collect or transmit: none

Tasbih+ has no user accounts and no servers. It requests no Internet permission and makes no network connections of its own. It contains no analytics, no advertising, and no tracking of any kind. Everything you do — your dhikr counts, settings, prayer-tracking log, Quran reading position and favourites, and any Zakat calculations you save — is stored only on your device and is deleted when you uninstall the app.

Location (optional, on-device only)

If you choose to use the calculated prayer-times or the Qibla compass feature, the app may ask for location permission (approximate and/or precise). Your location is used only on your device to:

  • calculate prayer times for where you are, and
  • work out the Qibla direction (the bearing to Makkah) for the compass.

Your location is never sent to us and never shared with anyone — we operate no servers to send it to. The app also works without location: you can use the bundled prayer timetable or enter a location manually. You can grant or revoke location permission at any time in Android settings.

Notifications, reminders and alarms

With your permission, Tasbih+ can show local notifications and alarms on your device, including: prayer-time and optional pre-prayer reminders, optional daily hadith/du’a, Islamic-date, fasting, Jumu’ah and Quran reading-plan reminders. To do this the app uses the notification, exact-alarm and (for the prayer tone) media-playback foreground-service permissions, and — if you enable the full-screen prayer alarm — permission to show that alarm over the lock screen. These features run entirely on your device using Android’s alarm scheduler; no reminder data leaves your phone. All of them are optional and off by default (except the basic in-app display), and can be turned off in the app’s settings. The app also re-arms your scheduled reminders after the device restarts.

Permissions we request — and why

  • Vibrate — tactile feedback for the counter and alerts.
  • Notifications — to show prayer-time and other reminders you enable.
  • Exact alarm & run after restart — to fire reminders at the right time and re-schedule them after a reboot.
  • Foreground service (media playback) — to play the prayer tone so it can finish and be stopped.
  • Full-screen intent — to show the prayer alarm over the lock screen (optional).
  • Approximate & precise location — optional; used on-device only for prayer times and Qibla, as described above.

The app requests no Internet, contacts, storage, microphone, camera, or advertising permissions.

Backup and sharing (you choose)

You can export your settings/counts to a file and import it later. This file is created only when you ask, and it is handed to whichever app you pick through Android’s standard share sheet — Tasbih+ does not upload it anywhere. Any Zakat privacy password you set is stored only as a one-way hash on your device; the password itself is never stored. If you have Google Backup enabled on your device, Android may include the app’s data in its own encrypted backup, which is governed by Google’s policy.

Children

The app is suitable for all ages and collects no data from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

Any updates to this policy will be posted on this page with a new effective date.
